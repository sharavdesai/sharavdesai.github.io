About

I am a Professor and Head of the Department of Pharmaceutical Biotechnology at Sanjivani College of Pharmaceutical Education and Research, Kopargaon, and a Fellow of the Institution of Chemists (India), with over 15 years of experience in bioinformatics, biotechnology, and academic leadership.

My research centres on the molecular biology of cancer — identifying novel therapeutic targets, building QSAR models, and running molecular docking and MD simulations (GROMACS, NAMD, Schrödinger Maestro) to understand protein–ligand interactions. I pair computational approaches with MTT, XTT, wound healing, and clonogenic assays on lung, breast, and liver cancer cell lines.

I hold 21 verified certifications from IBM, Google, Johns Hopkins, UC San Diego, Michigan, and Vanderbilt in data science, Python, ML, genomics, and cancer biology. In 2025, my group received a ₹28 Lakhs research grant from the Rajiv Gandhi Science & Technology Commission, Mumbai.

Research Areas

Target Identification

Oncogenic target discovery via subtractive genomics, network analysis, and structure-based reasoning.

MD Simulation

All-atom molecular dynamics (GROMACS, VMD, NAMD, Maestro) probing protein–ligand binding and conformational dynamics.

In Vitro Cytotoxicity

MTT, XTT, wound healing, clonogenic assays on NCI, MDA-MB, HEP3B cell lines to validate computational predictions.

QSAR & ML

Supervised ML and QSAR models in Python/R for biological activity prediction and lead optimization.

Virtual Screening

High-throughput docking (AutoDock, Vina, PyRx) of phytochemical and synthetic libraries against cancer targets.

Cancer Molecular Biology

Tumor microenvironment, cellular plasticity, drug resistance, sphingolipid signaling, immunotherapy targets.
