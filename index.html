About

I am a Professor and Head of the Department of Pharmaceutical Biotechnology at Sanjivani College of Pharmaceutical Education and Research, Kopargaon, with over 15 years of experience spanning bioinformatics, biotechnology, and academic leadership.

My research centres on the molecular biology of cancer — identifying and validating novel therapeutic targets, building QSAR models, and running molecular docking and molecular dynamics simulations to understand protein–ligand interactions at atomic resolution. I pair these computational approaches with wet-lab pharmacology, including MTT, XTT, and DNA fragmentation assays on lung and breast cancer cell lines.

I deploy simulation workloads on high-performance computing and cloud platforms (AWS EC2, SageMaker, Google Colab), and apply Python and R with machine learning to predictive modelling in drug discovery. My work has produced 65+ peer-reviewed publications, 12+ book chapters, and two filed patents in herbal formulations.

In 2025, my group received a research grant of ₹28 Lakhs from the Rajiv Gandhi Science & Technology Commission, Mumbai.

Research Areas

Target Identification

Discovery and prioritization of oncogenic targets through subtractive genomics, network analysis, and structure-based reasoning.

MD Simulation

All-atom molecular dynamics (VMD, NAMD, Schrödinger Maestro) probing binding stability and conformational dynamics of protein–ligand complexes.

In Vitro Cytotoxicity

MTT, XTT, and DNA fragmentation assays on lung and breast cancer cell lines (NCI, MDA-MB) to validate computational predictions.

QSAR & ML Modelling

Supervised machine learning and QSAR models in Python/R to predict biological activity and accelerate lead discovery.

Virtual Screening & Docking

High-throughput docking (AutoDock, Vina) of phytoconstituent and synthetic libraries against validated cancer targets.

Cancer Molecular Biology

Tumor microenvironment, sphingolipid signaling, immunotherapy targets, and nucleic-acid biomarkers underlying tumor progression.
